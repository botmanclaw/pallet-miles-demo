Priority growth lane +50% auto

Pickup window ≥ 4 hrs +10%
Flexible pickup windows let Warp batch your load with others — they save on truck utilization and share it back with you.
Delivery window ≥ 4 hrs +10%
Same logic as pickup — delivery flexibility reduces driver wait time and lets Warp optimize their last-mile routing.
MABD flexibility +15%
"Must Arrive By Date" flexibility means Warp can route your freight on the most efficient schedule rather than forcing an overnight push.
Booked 7+ days ahead +15% auto
Advance booking gives Warp time to consolidate loads and reduce empty miles. Better planning = lower cost = bigger credit for you.
2–3 pallets +10% auto
Multi-pallet shipments are more efficient to consolidate than single-pallet moves. Rewards you for shipping in meaningful quantities.
4+ pallets +25% auto
Larger loads dramatically improve truck fill rates. This is the biggest single efficiency gain Warp gets from a shipper — and they pass it on.
Pickup near Warp dock +10% auto
If you're close to a Warp cross-dock, they skip a relay stop. Fewer truck moves = lower cost = shared savings.
Dropoff near Warp dock +10% auto
Same as pickup — proximity on both ends compounds the efficiency gain.
Local trip < 60 mi +30% auto
Short urban hops are extremely valuable for network density — they're cheap to run but hard to fill. Warp rewards you heavily for them.

The Glide Path
Your deal only gets better over time.

Warp launches at a 20% markup cap — already below every traditional broker (25–35%). As the network densifies and Warp's real carrier costs drop, the cap ratchets down publicly each year: 20% → 16% → 13% → 11% → 10% by 2030. Each drop is a public event tied to quarterly margin milestones — not a promise, a ratchet. When Warp earns less on each load (because they're more efficient), your credit ceiling rises. Every improvement in their operations flows through to you.
